Supplements
Evidence-based recommendations
From Your Coach
These are the basics — nothing unnecessary. Always check with your doctor before adding anything new, especially given your health history.
DAILY STACK
Creatine Monohydrate
5g daily — any time of day
The most proven supplement in existence. More strength, more muscle over time. Safe long-term. Start Day 1 and don't stop. No loading phase needed.
Vitamin D3 + K2
2,000–5,000 IU D3 — with a meal
Most people are low without knowing it. Affects muscle function, mood, immune health, and recovery. Take with K2 for proper calcium absorption.
Magnesium Glycinate
300–400mg — before bed
Better sleep, less soreness. Particularly helpful given elevated stress levels. Glycinate form is easier on the stomach than other forms.
Fish Oil (Omega-3)
2–3g EPA+DHA daily — with food
Cuts systemic inflammation between sessions. Your joints will feel better and you'll recover faster. Look for EPA+DHA on the label, not just "fish oil."
AS NEEDED
Whey Protein
20–40g post-workout
Only if you're not hitting roughly 0.8–1g of protein per pound of bodyweight through food. Food always comes first. Use as a gap-filler, not a crutch.
Consult a physician before starting any new supplement, particularly given your health history.

Coaching Notes
Key reminders from your coach
From Your Coach
Stick to the plan. Track the lifts. Prioritize recovery. The results will follow.
NON-NEGOTIABLES
1
No barbell bench — ever
All horizontal chest pressing is DB-only. This is a permanent programming decision, not a temporary modification. DBs allow natural wrist and elbow tracking that protects you.
2
Neck tension check-in before every upper session
Rate your neck stiffness 1–10 before every Monday and Friday. If it hits 6 or above, drop upper body weights by 20% and skip any overhead pressing. Never train through neck pain.
3
Both active recovery days are mandatory
Tuesday and Saturday are not optional rest days — they're part of the program. With moderate-to-high stress and 3 hard lifting days plus a full body day, these sessions are how training becomes muscle.
4
Compound first, isolation second
Perform primary compound movements while you're freshest. Isolation work and accessories are finishers — never do them first.
TRAINING PRINCIPLES
5
Add weight over time
If you finish all your sets and reps easily, the weight goes up next session. That's the whole game. Aim for 5 lb increases on lower body, 2.5–5 lb on upper body DB work.
6
Track every session
Log every workout — exercise, sets, reps, weight used. Without tracking, progressive overload is guesswork. Use the log fields in this app every single session.
7
Rest periods matter
Use the prescribed rest times. Insufficient rest between heavy compound sets kills performance. The timer in each workout session is there for a reason — use it.
8
Watch for fatigue accumulation
If energy, strength, and motivation are all declining for 3+ sessions in a row, take a deload week immediately. Reduce loads 15–20%, drop 1 set per exercise. Keep all recovery blocks.
9
Show up consistently
A decent workout you showed up for beats a perfect workout you skipped. Every single time. The program only works if you work the program.
